Yale Ride Share App

PROBLEM

Students need a way to get from the airport to campus (and vice versa). Most options are expensive so the optimal strategy is to share a ride and split the cost.
Currently, most students use the Yale RideShare Facebook Group. The problem is that this group is not private and overloaded with text. It's difficult to dig through all of the posts to find someone with a similar ride schedule to you. On top of that, you may receive spam comments if you post.


There have been attempts at creating apps to solve these problems.
The first app is Ride Buddies, which requires a Yale login. While this solves the issue of privacy, the app design is poor and not well-marketed.
The other app is called Hopp. While this app has a more sophisticated interface, it is not Yale-specific enough. App store reviews explain that it is difficult to actually find a ride match.



MY SOLUTION

An app that offers privacy and specificity while using familiar interface styles.
I designed an app called Yale RideShare, which essentially combines interface elements from social apps, dating apps, and carpooling app. I was inspired by the booking features of Uber and Lyft, but I also wanted to incorporate the "matching" feature of Tinder and Bumble. Lastly, I wanted there to be a feed as there is on social media and the Facebook group. But this feed should look clean.
